Yoga Poses Chart

Yoga Pose Description
Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana) A foundational yoga pose that stretches the entire body, particularly the back, hamstrings, and shoulders. It also helps to calm the mind and improve focus.
Tree Pose (Vrikshasana) A balancing pose that strengthens the legs and improves focus and concentration. It also enhances hip flexibility and opens the chest.
Warrior II Pose (Virabhadrasana II) An energizing pose that builds strength in the legs and arms while opening the hips and chest. It improves stamina and balance.
Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana) A gentle backbend that stretches the spine, chest, and shoulders, while strengthening the back muscles. It also helps to relieve stress and fatigue.
Child's Pose (Balasana) A relaxing pose that stretches the hips, thighs, and ankles while reducing stress and fatigue. It promotes a sense of calm and relaxation.
Corpse Pose (Savasana) A restorative pose that relaxes the body and mind, promoting deep relaxation and rejuvenation. It helps to reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality.
Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana) A seated pose that stretches the spine, hamstrings, and shoulders. It calms the mind and helps to relieve stress and anxiety.
Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana) A backbend that strengthens the back, glutes, and hamstrings. It also improves spinal flexibility and stimulates the abdominal organs.
Triangle Pose (Trikonasana) A standing pose that stretches the hamstrings, groins, and hips. It also opens the chest and shoulders, improving balance and stability.
Extended Side Angle Pose (Utthita Parsvakonasana) An intense stretch for the legs, groins, and sides of the torso. It strengthens the legs and improves stamina.
Half Moon Pose (Ardha Chandrasana) A balancing pose that strengthens the legs and core muscles. It improves coordination and focus.
Boat Pose (Navasana) An abdominal strengthener that improves balance and posture. It also stimulates the kidneys and thyroid gland.
